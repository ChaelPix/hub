Regional Competitionü•à

I'm a Worlskills Competitor in Autonomous Mobile Robotics selected for the French Team for the WORLDS 2024.

All begins with the regional competition, in teams of 2. We had 4 trials:

- A sand course where we were forbidden to use caterpillar tracks on the robot.
- a little maze without external walls.
- a sloppy ramp.
- completing the 3 courses in a row.

For each trial, the robot had a water cup to deliver to a random colored cup located at the end of the track with a claw.

The competition lasts 3 days. We built 2 robots, one for the sand course and the other one for the other courses.
I programmed the robots with C++ for Arduino.


I wasn't expected to participate to this competition. Indeed, I was originally a volunteer to help to present the skill
of mobile robotics. But, in the morning of the competition, a competitor abandoned and didn't come. I had the opportunity to replace him.

So, I had a go, and learnt everything during the 3 days of competition, then won a Silver Medal!

National Competitionü•à

The competition was about a storage factory simulation.

In a 4*2m area with walls. The robot has to navigate on the board to stamp boxes identified by QR code.
For this competition, I didn't use Arduino, but I had to learn ROS1 (robot operating system) by myself. Indeed, this operating system
is optimised for controlling robots and helped me use the Lidar for the localization of the robot on
the board. It's a powerful tool with a big community.

Despite some unexpected hardware issues, we finished 2nd with a silver medal.

However, since we had already learnt ROS1 and showed that we're very passionate, motivated, and had more potential than gold
medal winners, we had been chosen for the French Team for the Worlds!

Austria Competition üèÜ

A few weeks after having been picked for the French Team, we had the opportunity to
participate as a country guest in Austria's national competition.

For this competition and the worlds' one, we have to use a new robot kit from Studica.
So we had to learn how to use the studica's libraries and migrate our knowledge about ROS1 to ROS2.

The competition was about localization and picking objects.

In a 4*2m area with walls. The robot has to navigate on the board only with ultrasonic and InfraRed sensors.
This competition was an opportunity to test our new tools, like our Docker, our programme systems, and so on.

We had 3 intense days where we learnt a lot of things that will help us in our Worlds' competition preparation.
