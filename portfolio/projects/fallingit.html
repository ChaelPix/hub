After working on several Unity projects, I decided to challenge myself by developing a complete Android mobile game, from concept to publishing on the Google Play Store.

Game Core

Falling It is a casual puzzle game where players must strategize to make an apple fall to the ground by interacting with the environment and leveraging realistic 3D physics.


FallingIt Game Core

As extra-core, there are limited time events with special rewards. And collectible for the character and garden decoration.


Customizing Apple and Garden


Event page with special and limited features

I also learnt to use push-notifications.


Push notifications

Play Store Publication

This was my first experience publishing a game on the Google Play Store. I learned how to sign an app, manage XML dependencies in Android Studio, and navigate the overall publication process.


FallingIt on Playstore

Google Play Games Features Integration

To enhance the user experience and explore the Google Play Games SDK, I implemented Play Cloud to save player progress and Play Leaderboard to encourage competition among players.


Login with Google Play Games. It synchronises the data with the cloud

Loading and showing a leaderboard

In-App Purchases (IAP)

I added in-app purchase functionality to understand its configuration and implementation, providing players with additional features while gaining insight into monetization strategies.


Working IAP in-game

Asset Bundle Delivery

To introduce new maps, events, and garden objects without requiring frequent app updates, I integrated Unity Cloud with asset bundles. This allowed me to deliver fresh content directly from the cloud, improving the player experience and streamlining game updates.
